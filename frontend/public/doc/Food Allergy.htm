Food Allergies

A food allergy occurs when the immune system mistakenly treats a certain food as dangerous. Antibodies to the allergen trigger the release of chemicals like histamine, causing symptoms that affect various parts of the body.

What's a Reaction Like?

For example, in a peanut allergy, antibodies react to peanuts, causing symptoms such as:

  • Tightness in the throat
  • Hoarse voice
  • Wheezing
  • Cough
  • Nausea, vomiting, or diarrhea
  • Hives or tingling in the lips or tongue

In severe cases, a food allergy can cause anaphylaxis, a life-threatening condition where multiple body systems react at once, potentially leading to blood pressure drops, airway constriction, and swelling of the tongue.

How Do You Know If You Have One?

Identifying a food allergy can be difficult, especially when multiple ingredients are involved. Doctors may conduct allergy tests, review family history, or perform diagnostic tests like skin testing with extracts of food allergens to observe the body’s reaction.

What Will the Doctor Do?

If a doctor suspects a food allergy, they may refer you to an allergist. The allergist will ask about your reactions and family history, and may perform skin or blood tests to confirm allergies.

How Are Food Allergies Diagnosed?

To diagnose a food allergy, the allergist may use:

  • Skin Tests: Small amounts of food extracts are applied to the skin to check for redness or swelling.
  • Blood Tests: Measures specific antibodies to identify potential food allergens.

How Are Food Allergies Treated?

There is no cure for food allergies, but they can be managed through:

  • Avoidance: Avoid trigger foods to prevent reactions.
  • Emergency Plan: Carry epinephrine (an adrenaline shot) for severe reactions like anaphylaxis.
  • Antihistamines: Used for mild symptoms, but they cannot replace epinephrine for severe cases.

Living With Food Allergies

Living with food allergies requires caution but doesn’t have to limit your lifestyle. Many companies now offer allergen-free substitutes for favorite foods, making management easier.
