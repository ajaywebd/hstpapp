Autism Spectrum Disorder

Autism spectrum disorder (ASD) is a developmental issue affecting a child’s nervous system, growth, and development, typically becoming apparent within the first three years of life. Children with ASD often appear detached, may lack social awareness, prefer routines, and have difficulties communicating or making eye contact.

ASD can hinder social skills development, partly because children with ASD may not interpret facial expressions or emotions. Children with ASD may:

  • Not want to be touched
  • Prefer solitary play
  • Resist changes in routine

Some children with ASD may excel in specific tasks like counting or remembering details and may show talents in areas like art or music.

Causes of ASD in a Child

The exact causes of ASD are unknown. Genetic factors, brain structure irregularities, or chemical imbalances may contribute. ASD is not caused by parenting methods.

Less common causes:

  • Exposure to toxins before or after birth
  • Complications during delivery
  • Infections before birth

Risk Factors

ASD is more common in boys than girls, with genetic conditions like Fragile-X, PKU, neurofibromatosis, tuberous sclerosis, and chromosome issues raising the risk. Genetic testing by a medical geneticist can assess specific gene-related risks.

Symptoms

Each child may have slightly different symptoms. Below are the most common symptoms of ASD.

Social Symptoms

  • Difficulty with eye contact
  • Challenges in making friends or interacting with peers

Communication Symptoms

  • Poor communication skills
  • Delayed speech or no speech
  • Echolalia or repeating phrases from media

Behavioral Symptoms

  • Repetitive movements like rocking or flapping
  • Increased or reduced sensitivity to sensory input
  • Strict routines and rituals

ASD symptoms may resemble other conditions, so professional diagnosis is essential.

Diagnosis

ASD is typically diagnosed before age 2 through well-child screenings and by monitoring certain developmental milestones:

  • No babbling, pointing, or gesturing by 12 months
  • No single words by 16 months
  • No two-word phrases by 24 months, only repeating others' words
  • Loss of language or social skills at any age
  • Lack of eye contact by 3-4 months

Additional tests for ASD may include nervous system exams, imaging tests, mental health assessments, and genetic testing.

Treatment

Each child with ASD needs a customized treatment program, ideally started as early as possible with parental involvement.

  • Behavior change programs: Focus on social, motor, and cognitive skills, and address behavior.
  • Special education programs: Develop social, speech, language, self-care, and vocational skills.
  • Medicine: Some children benefit from medications to manage symptoms.

A mental health provider can also offer support to both the child and family, including counseling, social skills training, and one-on-one therapy.

Prevention

Experts do not know how to prevent ASD, and it is unrelated to parenting. However, early diagnosis and treatment can significantly improve quality of life and developmental outcomes.

Living with Autism

The primary care provider will be instrumental in managing ASD treatment and understanding care requirements. Parents play a critical role in supporting their child by:

  • Keeping all appointments with the child’s healthcare provider
  • Collaborating with a care team that may include specialists like neurologists, therapists, and social workers
  • Working with schools and healthcare providers to develop a treatment plan
  • Seeking support from community services and connecting with other parents of children with ASD

Key Points

  • ASD affects a child's nervous system, growth, and development.
  • Communication and social skills are often impaired.
  • Genetic factors may contribute to ASD.
  • All children should be screened for ASD by age 2.
  • Diagnosis may involve imaging and genetic tests.
  • Children with ASD require a specialized treatment plan, which may include behavior modification and social skills training.
