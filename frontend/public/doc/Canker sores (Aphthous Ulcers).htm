Canker Sores (Aphthous Ulcers)

Canker sores (aphthous ulcers) are small sores inside the mouth. They are often found inside the lips, on the cheeks, or on the tongue.

Causes

The exact cause is not known, but many factors are thought to be involved with the development of canker sores. These include:

  • Weakened immune system
  • Allergies to food, such as coffee, chocolate, cheese, nuts, and citrus fruits
  • Stress
  • Viruses and bacteria
  • Injury to the mouth
  • Poor nutrition
  • Certain medicines
  • Irritation from orthodontic braces

Canker sores are usually first seen in children and teens between the ages of 10 and 19. For about 3 in 10 children affected, canker sores reoccur for years after the first outbreak. They are not contagious.

Canker sores that keep coming back may be related to conditions such as celiac disease, inflammatory bowel disease, or HIV infection.

Symptoms

Each child may experience symptoms differently. The most common symptoms of aphthous stomatitis include:

  • Painful sores in the mouth, usually inside the lips, on the cheeks, or on the tongue
  • Sores covered with a yellow layer and having a red base
  • Trouble eating or talking because of the sores
  • No fever in most cases

Canker sores usually heal in 7 to 14 days but tend to recur.

Diagnosis

Your child’s healthcare provider can usually diagnose canker sores with a complete health history and physical exam. Additional tests may be performed to rule out other causes, including:

  • Blood tests
  • Cultures of the sores
  • Biopsy of the sore, where a small tissue sample is examined under a microscope

Treatment

Treatment depends on your child’s symptoms, age, and overall health. It also considers the severity of the condition. The goal of treatment is to ease symptoms and may include:

  • Drinking more fluids
  • Taking acetaminophen for any fever or pain
  • Maintaining proper oral hygiene
  • Using topical medicines to ease pain
  • Using mouth rinses to help with the pain

Avoiding spicy, salty, or acidic foods can also help reduce mouth irritation and improve comfort.
